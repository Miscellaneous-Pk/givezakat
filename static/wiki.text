					<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="February_2020">February 2020</span></h3>
					<p>On 26 February, <a href="/wiki/Zafar_Mirza" title="Zafar Mirza">Zafar Mirza</a>, the Prime Minister's Special Assistant on Health, stated in a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweet</a>: "I can confirm first two cases of
						coronavirus in Pakistan. Both cases are being taken care of according to clinical standard protocols &amp; both of them are stable."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>The first patient was a student at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Karachi" title="University of Karachi">University of Karachi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sindh_province"
							class="mw-redirect" title="Sindh province">Sindh province</a>. The second patient is from the federal territory of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Both patients had
						recently returned from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>Within a week of the first two cases, Pakistan confirmed three more cases, including a case in Pakistan's capital, <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">Islamabad</a> and also in <a href="/wiki/Rawalpindi"
							title="Rawalpindi">Rawalpindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">Punjab</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="March_2020">March 2020</span></h3>
					<p>The fifth case was also reported, on 2 March, from the federal area of the country,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> of a 45-year-old woman from <a href="/wiki/Gilgit" title="Gilgit">Gilgit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baltistan" title="Baltistan">Baltistan</a>, who had also travelled from Iran.<sup
							id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 6 March, <a href="/wiki/Murtaza_Wahab" title="Murtaza Wahab">Murtaza Wahab</a> announced that the first patient in Karachi had fully recovered and was later discharged from hospital after testing negative.<sup id="cite_ref-16"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 8 March, Pakistan confirmed its seventh case of COVID-19, also in Karachi.<sup
							id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>The next day, Pakistan reported nine new cases in Karachi, meaning that there was a total of 16 cases of COVID-19, with Sindh having the highest number of cases, 13 altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Five of the new patients had travelled to <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and some others patients had returned from <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Three new
						cases were confirmed on 10 March, including one in <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad,_Sindh" title="Hyderabad, Sindh">Hyderabad</a> and the first case in <a href="/wiki/Quetta" title="Quetta">Quetta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balochistan"
							title="Balochistan">Balochistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 11 March, 76 suspected cases were reported in several districts of Punjab province, including <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gujranwala" title="Gujranwala">Gujranwala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sargodha"
							title="Sargodha">Sargodha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hafizabad" title="Hafizabad">Hafizabad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lodhran" title="Lodhran">Lodhran</a>. The Healthcare Department officials informed that ten patients were immediately cleared of
						the suspicion, while 55 patients were cleared after testing negative. The test result of the remaining 11 suspected patients is still awaited.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>Also on that day, a second case in <a href="/wiki/Gilgit-Baltistan" title="Gilgit-Baltistan">Gilgit-Baltistan</a> was confirmed in <a href="/wiki/Skardu" title="Skardu">Skardu</a>. The patient is a 14-year-old.<sup id="cite_ref-23"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 12 March, there was a third case found in Gilgit-Baltistan, a 31-year-old resident of <a href="/wiki/Shigar_District" title="Shigar District">Shigar district</a>, who had a travel history of Iran. The spokesman said that the patient
						is under treatment at the Skardu hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On the same day, <a href="/wiki/Murtaza_Wahab" title="Murtaza Wahab">Murtaza Wahab</a> informed that the
						second patient of the virus have been completely recovered.
					</p>
					<p>On 13 March, the Sindh Health Department identified a 52-year-old patient as positive, which marked the first case of local disease transmission, as the patient had travelled from <a href="/wiki/Islamabad"
							title="Islamabad">Islamabad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> 24 of the 27 suspected cases in
						<a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkwa</a> were also cleared that day.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the day, the total number
						of cases had risen to 28, with seven new cases in <a href="/wiki/Taftan,_Balochistan" title="Taftan, Balochistan">Taftan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 14 March, the number of cases rose to 33. Three new patients were found positive in Karachi while one was reported in Islamabad. Later, it was also reported that a woman who was found infected in Islamabad has transitted the virus to
						her husband as well.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 15 March, five more cases were announced in Karachi, including a second local transmission of the coronavirus in Sindh, while the other three had a travel history of <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>
						and one had a local travel history of Balochistan.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> There was also a new case reported in <a href="/wiki/Islamabad_Capital_Territory"
							title="Islamabad Capital Territory">Islamabad Capital Territory</a>.
					</p>
					<p>The news cases were confirmed by Sindh government spokesperson Murtaza Wahab in a tweet. “More results have come in. So far 50 people who had arrived in Sukkur from Taftaan have tested positive, 25 at Karachi and 1 at Hyderabad. So the
						total patients have reached 76 in Sindh. Out of these 76 patients, 2 have recovered and the remaining 74 are being kept in isolation,” he said. Earlier in the day, the Sindh health department had reported four new cases in Karachi.
					</p>
					<p>Meanwhile, Lahore Health Secretary Mohammad Usman confirmed the first case of coronavirus was found in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">Punjab</a>. The infected
						patient came back from <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on 10 March 2020 and was shifted to Meo Hospital in an isolation ward.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On the same day, another 13 tests from <a href="/wiki/Sukkur" title="Sukkur">Sukkur</a> came positive from the pilgrims that came back from Iran at the Taftan border and were kept in
						quarantine after being suspected cases.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> This brought the total of 53 in Pakistan, while 15 March 2020 has recorded the highest number of people tested
						positive to date, with a total of 19, in a single day so far.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 16 March, 83 new cases of virus were confirmed raising the toll to 136.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Of these new cases, 15 were confirmed in <a href="/wiki/Dera_Ismail_Khan"
							title="Dera Ismail Khan">Dera Ismail Khan</a>. These had returned from Taftan and were kept in quarantine in <a href="/wiki/Darazinda" class="mw-redirect" title="Darazinda">Darazinda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dera_Ismail_Khan"
							title="Dera Ismail Khan">Dera Ismail Khan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>A total of 119 positive cases in Sukkur were confirmed on 17 March.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> 52 new cases were announced. The total number of cases in Pakistan rose to 237.<sup
							id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 18 March, the first case of coronavirus was reported from <a href="/wiki/Azad_Kashmir" title="Azad Kashmir">Azad Kashmir</a>. The first two deaths caused by coronavirus in Pakistan were also confirmed. Both cases were reported from
						the province of <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkuwa</a>, one in <a href="/wiki/Peshawar" title="Peshawar">Peshawar</a> and the other one in <a href="/wiki/Mardan_District"
							title="Mardan District">Mardan District</a>.
					</p>
					<p>The first person to die because of coronavirus was a 50-year-old who had recently returned to Mardan after performing Umrah in <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi
							Arabia</a>. The second victim was a 36-year-old from KP's <a href="/wiki/Hangu_District" class="mw-disambig" title="Hangu District">Hangu District</a> and passed away at a hospital in Peshawar. The minister reports that the man had
						recently come back to Pakistan from <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>.
					</p>
					<p>On 19 March, the total number of coronavirus cases reached 384, and by the end of the day, coronavirus cases had rose to a total of 452.
					</p>

					<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_response">Government response</span></h2>
					<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal">Federal</span></h3>
					<p>After confirmed reports of hundreds of cases in neighbouring <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Civil_Aviation_Authority" title="Pakistan Civil Aviation Authority">Pakistan Civil Aviation
							Authority</a> (CAA) as a security step introduced screening measures at four major airports: <a href="/wiki/Islamabad_International_Airport" title="Islamabad International Airport">Islamabad</a>, <a
							href="/wiki/Jinnah_International_Airport" title="Jinnah International Airport">Karachi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allama_Iqbal_International_Airport" title="Allama Iqbal International Airport">Lahore</a> and <a
							href="/wiki/Bacha_Khan_International_Airport" title="Bacha Khan International Airport">Peshawar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>Pakistan also closed its border to travellers from <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Wesh%E2%80%93Chaman_border_crossing" title="Wesh–Chaman border crossing">Chaman border</a> (since 2
						March) and from <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> (until 7 March; and later from 16 March) at the <a href="/wiki/Taftan,_Balochistan" title="Taftan, Balochistan">Taftan border</a>, after 43 cases were reported during the <a
							href="/wiki/2020_coronavirus_pandemic_in_Iran" title="2020 coronavirus pandemic in Iran">coronavirus pandemic in Iran</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
						It will also close all land borders, including the <a href="/wiki/China%E2%80%93Pakistan_border" title="China–Pakistan border">China-Pakistan border</a> with <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> from 16 March.<sup
							id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>On 13 March, President <a href="/wiki/Arif_Alvi" title="Arif Alvi">Dr. Arif Alvi</a> in a special tweet, advised the public to avoid participation in mass gatherings, handshaking or hugging in addition to taking other precautionary
						measures if they observe symptoms of flu or corona virus infection.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>Also on that day, <a href="/wiki/Shafqat_Mahmood" title="Shafqat Mahmood">Shafqat Mahmood</a> that at the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Council_(Pakistan)" title="National Security Council (Pakistan)">National Security Council
							meeting</a> with <a href="/wiki/Imran_Khan" title="Imran Khan">PM Imran Khan</a>, it was decided that all schools and universities would be closed until 5 April.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> However, the faculty and staff will go to work as usual. The lectures/classes will be held online in some institutions i.e., Riphah International University, FAST NUCES University, Institute
						of Space Technology.
					</p>
					<p>It was also announced that Pakistan would completely seal its <a href="/wiki/Durand_Line" title="Durand Line">land border with Afghanistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taftan,_Balochistan" title="Taftan, Balochistan">its land border with
							Iran</a> from 16 March for at least two weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>From 13 March, Pakistan stopped all international flights, except those at <a href="/wiki/Islamabad_International_Airport" title="Islamabad International Airport">Islamabad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jinnah_International_Airport"
							title="Jinnah International Airport">Karachi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Allama_Iqbal_International_Airport" title="Allama Iqbal International Airport">Lahore</a> airports.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provincial">Provincial</span></h3>
					<p>The government of Pakistan's largest province, <a href="/wiki/Balochistan,_Pakistan" title="Balochistan, Pakistan">Balochistan</a>, started taking steps to prevent the spread of this virus. The provincial government constituted a
						14-member technical committee.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p><a href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Sindh" title="Chief Minister of Sindh">Chief Minister of Sindh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syed_Murad_Ali_Shah" title="Syed Murad Ali Shah">Syed Murad Ali Shah</a>, announced the closure of all educational
						institutes in Sindh until 30 March, which was later extended until 30 May.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Sindh" title="Government of Sindh">The Government of Sindh</a> also announced that the remaining <a href="/wiki/2020_Pakistan_Super_League"
							title="2020 Pakistan Super League">PSL matches</a> at <a href="/wiki/National_Stadium,_Karachi" title="National Stadium, Karachi">National Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> would be held <a
							href="/wiki/Behind_closed_doors_(sport)" title="Behind closed doors (sport)">behind closed doors</a>, on 12 March.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
						The Sindh Government imposed a temporary ban on marriage halls, lawns, banquets, piyala hotels and tea stalls functioning after 23:15 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p><a href="/wiki/Government_of_Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Government of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Government of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a> also announced to close all educational institutes until 31 March and postponed all festivals and events until
						further notice as a preventive measure, though no confirmed case has been reported yet in <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The teaching staff will also not be permitted to enter schools, colleges and universities.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p><a href="/wiki/Government_of_Gilgit-Baltistan" title="Government of Gilgit-Baltistan">Gilgit-Baltistan Government</a> declared medical emergency in the province after more cases of <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019"
							title="Coronavirus disease 2019">coronavirus</a> reported. Educational institutes are further closed until 31 March as a preventive measure.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Day" title="Pakistan Day">Pakistan Day Parade</a>, scheduled to be held on 23 March, was cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effect_on_sports">Effect on sports</span></h2>
					<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Impact_of_the_2019%E2%80%9320_coronavirus_pandemic_on_sports#Cricket" title="Impact of the 2019–20 coronavirus pandemic on sports">Impact of the 2019–20
							coronavirus pandemic on sports §&#160;Cricket</a></div>
					<p>Fears of the <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">coronavirus disease</a> spreading alarmed the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Sindh" title="Government of Sindh">Sindh provincial government</a>, who
						announced on 13 March that remaining matches of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Pakistan_Super_League" title="2020 Pakistan Super League">2020 Pakistan Super League (PSL)</a>, scheduled in <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a>,
						would be played <a href="/wiki/Behind_closed_doors_(sport)" title="Behind closed doors (sport)">behind closed doors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Cricket_Board"
							title="Pakistan Cricket Board">Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB)</a> offered international players participating in PSL to leave for their home countries (if they wish so), while PSL continues as per schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-65"
							class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> 10 international players and one international staff member opted to leave the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2020_Pakistan_Super_League_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Pakistan Super League Final">Final match</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a>, was
						rescheduled to 18 March instead of 22 March.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>As on 17th March 2020, The knockout fixtures (Both Semi Finals and Final) of Pakistan Super League (PSL) have been indefinitely postponed due to the sudden spike in coronavirus cases in Pakistan.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a
								href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
					</p>
					<p>After six years, when <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_League" title="Football Federation League">Pakistan Football Federation League</a> returned, the matches were played before 16 March 2020. On 16 March 2020, the <a
							href="/wiki/Pakistan_Football_Federation" title="Pakistan Football Federation">Pakistan Football Federation</a> released a notification announcing that the matches were postponed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact"
							style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
					</p>
